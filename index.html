About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

With an indigenous population of about 20,000, Taniti offers visitors an authentic cultural experience alongside natural wonders. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Travel Information

Getting Around

  • Public buses serve Taniti City (5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily)
  • Private buses serve the rest of the island
  • Taxis available in Taniti City
  • Rental cars available near the airport
  • Bikes and helmets for rent (helmets required by law)

Good to Know

  • Power outlets are 120 volts (same as United States)
  • U.S. dollar is the main currency (euros and yen also accepted)
  • Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English
  • One hospital and several clinics available with multilingual staff
  • Violent crime is very rare but be aware of pickpocketing

Frequently Asked Questions

General Information

  • Power outlets are 120 volts (same as United States)
  • U.S. dollar is the primary currency
    • Euros and yen also accepted
    • Major credit cards widely accepted
    • Currency exchange available at several banks
  • Language
    • Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English
    • Limited English in rural areas, especially among older residents

Safety & Services

  • Healthcare
    • One hospital with multilingual staff
    • Several medical clinics available
  • Safety
    • Violent crime is very rare
    • Be aware of occasional pickpocketing and petty crimes in tourist areas

Local Regulations

  • Alcohol Service
    • No alcohol sales between midnight and 9:00 a.m.
    • Legal drinking age is 18 (not strictly enforced)
  • Holiday Operations
    • Many tourist attractions and restaurants close during national holidays
    • Plan your visit accordingly around local holidays
